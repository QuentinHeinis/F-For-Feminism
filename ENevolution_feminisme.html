Evolution of Feminism around the World

Feminism has for each Country a place less or more important. From one place to another around the world, Feminism will be developed at another scale. This scale will vary in times and impacts.

Feminism in the United States

In the United States, the laws about women's status came from the British regime. It is a doctrine which puts the woman under the cover of the identity of the husband. A philosophy which should protect women. However, the autonomy of the woman is limited. The husband has all the authority on the woman, it is hard for them to get things going forwards. But They finally succeeded to elaborate the basics to start a political battle.

It is in the 60/70’s that the Woman’s Rights increased. The laws that was supposed to protect the women are now considered as discrimination. American rights became closer to the principle of universal declaration of human rights.

However, the doctrine said before leaves its trace. Indeed, domestic violences and rights about reproduction persists. Every minutes, 24 personnes are victims of rape, domestic violences or stalking...

Feminism in France

In France, one of the first feminist is Olympe de Gouges. She published in 1791, the Declaration of woman’s rights and the citizen, the first article is saying the woman born free and stays equal to the man’s rights. However, in 1804, the civil code of Napoleon subjects the woman legally to her husband. Then, under the 3rd republic, in 1870, women could start having rights.They first got access to instruction, then rights about work and access to many works.

It is only in the 50’s that womens gots many more rights. To start in 1945, women were able to votes as same as men. Then in 1946, the equality between women and men was written in the Constitution. It was only in 1991 that the first woman, Edith Cression, in France got elected as 1st minister.

In the beginning of the XXIe century, women had access to rights like the non-prescription contraceptives in cases of urgences or better equality between men and women in terms of work.

Feminism in Japan

Between 1868 and 1912 in Japan, thanks to industrialisation and urbanisation, the authority of the Husband on women decreased. However, in 1898, the new civil code denied the rights of women. Which has the effect of submitting the women under the men.

After the 2nd world war, women had access to rights like voting. In 1947, the clause about equality between men and women was included in the Constitution and the civil code. In 1986, the law about equality of luck also took effect. But also today, most of the works are still getting segmented between men and women. Especially jobs like receptionist, medical secretary and cashier.
