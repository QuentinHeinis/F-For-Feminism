Timeline

A timeline from the 1940s to the present day listing some of the events related to women in the three countries cited in the previous article.

1940

1945: Women are allowed to vote.

1960

1965: For married women, the pill becomes definitively legal in all 50 states only after a Supreme Court decision.
1967: The AAU prohibits women from competing with male runners.

1964: Menie Grégoire published "Le Métier de femme", a plea for equality in the couple and contraception.
1964: The television program "Cherchez la femme" by Eliane Victor, which focuses on the difficulty for women to reach positions of responsibility, is broadcast.
1964: The National Council of the Reformed Church of France declared contraceptive methods legitimate.
1965: Tillion participated in a meeting of experts on the "Promotion of Women" at UNESCO in Paris.
1965: The law reforms matrimonial regimes: a married woman can work and open a bank account without her husband's consent.
1967: The National Assembly passed the Neuwirth law authorizing oral contraception but prohibiting its advertising.

1970

1970: Ruth Bader Ginsburg co-founded the "Women's Rights Law Reporter", the first American newspaper to focus exclusively on women's rights.
1971: The Supreme Court renders its first decision against sexual discrimination in hiring.
1972: The Supreme Court recognized the access of unmarried women to the contraceptive pill.
1972: The Senate passed the Equal Rights Amendment.

1970 : Esterel presents its unisex collection.
1970: The law replaces the notion of "head of family" with joint parental authority.
1970: The association "Laissez-les-vivre" is formed by Dr. Lejeune to fight abortion.
1971: A manifesto signed by 343 women was published by "Le Nouvel Observateur": it demanded access to contraception and the right to abortion.
1971: The MLF marches 3k women in Paris for free abortion and contraception.
1972: The law introduced equal pay for men and women.
1974: The National Assembly passed the Neuwirth Law, which allowed the reimbursement and free sale of contraception to minors.
1974: The National Assembly passed the law on voluntary interruption of pregnancy, championed by Health Minister Veil. It authorized abortion during the first 10 weeks of pregnancy.
1975: The Veil law authorizing abortion was enacted (for 5 years).
1975: The National Assembly adopted a bill authorizing divorce "by mutual consent".
1975: The National Assembly adopted a bill prohibiting all sexist discrimination in hiring.

1980

1981 : Sojourner Truth entre dans le "National Women's Hall of Fame".
1984: Geraldine Ferraro became the first woman to be nominated as a (Democratic) candidate for Vice President of the United States.
1987: The Supreme Court ruled that U.S. Rotary clubs must be open to women.

1980: The National Assembly passed a bill prohibiting the dismissal of a pregnant woman.
1980: Rape is recognized as a crime by law.
1982 : A Women's Information Center (CDIF) opens its doors at 3 place Danton in Le Havre.
1982: Abortion is reimbursed by the Social Security.
1983: The Law on Professional Equality prohibits any discrimination on the basis of sex in professional life.

1990

1999: Contraception is decriminalized.

1991: Law 91-73 authorizes advertising for condoms and certain other contraceptives.
1999: Congress approved the constitutional law allowing the legislator to take measures to promote equality between women and men.

2000

2000 : Frances Allen commits to the promotion of women in IT.
2006: UNICEF, in its annual report, states that improving the lot of the world's children involves eliminating discrimination against women and empowering them.

2001: The law allows private doctors to prescribe contraceptives to minors without parental authorization.

2002: Women-only cars during rush hour were introduced on the Midosuji line of the Osaka subway.
2004: Women-only cars were introduced all day on weekdays on the Midosuji line of the Osaka subway.

2010

2010: Former Chilean President Bachelet becomes Deputy Secretary-General of the UN, in charge of the promotion of women.
2013: At the UN podium, Malala Yousafzai speaks about access to education for girls: "Extremists are afraid of books and pens. The power of education scares them.
2016 : Supreme Court rules Texas law restricting access to abortion illegal.
2017: Schiappa is appointed Secretary of State for Equality between Men and Women.

2010 : The policewoman Aurélie Fouquet (26) is hit by 3 bullets during a shooting and dies (1st municipal policewoman killed on duty).
2011: At the initiative of Blondin, a bill "to remove obsolete provisions relating to women's rights" is registered in the Senate.
2013 : The law prohibiting women from wearing pants is repealed.

2020

2020: Christina Koch, back from the ISS, lands: she breaks the record for the longest stay in space for a woman.
2021 : Arkansas bans abortion, hoping to overturn the 1973 legalization.

2021 : D'Amarzit is appointed CEO of the Paris Stock Exchange.
2021 : The National Assembly passes medically assisted reproduction (MAP) for all women.
2021 : Josephine Baker will enter the Pantheon on November 30 (first black woman).
2021 : Contraception will now be free for French women up to age 25.

2021: Yoshiro Mori, the boss of the Tokyo Olympics, resigns after his sexist remarks.
